# Earning LLAx

## ——How Real Participation Becomes Redeemable Equity

This document explains, from the user’s side, how LLAx is earned across the ecosystem, what each path rewards, and why the design treats some contributions as worth more than others. It is the companion to the Economic Model: where that document explains what LLAx is, this one explains how you come to hold it.

## 1. Before the Channels: What LLAx Is For

LLAx is the behavioral incentive token of the LinkLayerAI ecosystem. It exists to measure real participation and turn it into redeemable equity. You do not buy LLAx on a market, and you cannot acquire it through secondary trading; it is earned, and earned only by doing things that genuinely contribute to the protocol — contributing verifiable trading data, paying for and using Agent services, and bringing real users into the ecosystem.

Whatever way you earn it, LLAx points in one direction. It can be used as fuel to pay for select platform services, and it can be converted — one way only — into LLA, the protocol’s value and governance token. That conversion permanently burns the LLAx involved. This is the single fact worth holding in mind as you read about the individual channels: every path below is, ultimately, a path from real behavior toward a share of long-term protocol value.

## 2. The Five Ways to Earn LLAx

There are five channels through which LLAx is earned. Each is independent, each rewards a distinct kind of contribution, and each is capped by its own allocation so that no single channel can exhaust the whole supply. The channels are not ranked by convenience but by the value of what they ask of you — and that ordering of value is itself part of the design, explained in the next section.

### Channel One — Purchasing Platform Services

When you purchase a platform service package, LLAx is granted to you as an attached benefit of that purchase. This is the most direct path: you are paying for a real service the protocol provides, and the LLAx accompanies it. Because this channel is tied to genuine revenue rather than to a behavior the protocol must verify after the fact, the LLAx arrives in full the moment your payment confirms, with no lock-up and no vesting. It is the channel with the least friction, by design — revenue-generating participation should not be made to wait.

### Channel Two — Contributing Historical Trading Data (One-Time)

When you connect a read-only exchange API, the protocol can look back over your recent contract trading history and reward you for the verifiable risk events it contains — the liquidations and loss-taking exits that record real decisions made under real pressure. This is a one-time reward, issued once the data is verified, and it scales with the density of what your history contains. A trader whose record holds more of these real risk events has contributed denser, more valuable training data, and is rewarded on a higher tier accordingly.

It is worth being clear about why this is so. The events being rewarded are not rewarded because losing is good. They are rewarded because a record of how real risk unfolded — where leverage broke, where a position was abandoned, where a boundary was crossed — is exactly the high-information data from which an Agent learns to serve a trader like you. Your history, including its hardest moments, is the training set.

### Channel Three — Continuous Data Authorization (Monthly)

Connecting your API once earns the historical reward; keeping it connected earns an ongoing one. As long as your authorization remains active, the protocol reviews each new period of risk events and issues a recurring monthly reward for your continued contribution. This channel exists because a continuous, incremental stream of data is worth more to the protocol than a single historical snapshot — a living record of how your trading evolves over time is what allows an Agent to keep calibrating to your behavior rather than to a frozen past.

This is why the design weights ongoing contribution as it does, and why the monthly reward is structured as a continuation of the historical one rather than a way to surpass it. The intent is to reward sustained, genuine participation — not to create an incentive to manufacture activity. Continuity is the contribution being paid for.

### Channel Four — Referring Real Users

When you bring a new user into the ecosystem through your referral, and that user becomes a real, paying participant, you earn LLAx in proportion to their genuine spend. This is a deliberately modest channel. It rewards only a single level — the people your referrals go on to refer earn you nothing — so that the structure cannot drift into the multi-level shape that referral schemes so often degrade into. And it is capped per account, so that referral is a healthy contribution to growth rather than a profession unto itself. The reward attaches to real users doing real things, never to the act of signing up names.

### Channel Five — Holding Snapshot on the BNB Chain

When you sign in with your wallet, the protocol checks — quietly, in the background — whether that wallet holds assets on the BNB Chain, and if it does, grants you a one-time fixed amount of LLAx. This is the lowest-barrier channel, and it exists for a specific reason: to welcome the native BNB-chain trading community into the ecosystem and lower the threshold for a first-time participant to begin. The amount is fixed regardless of how much you hold; what is being recognized is not the size of your holdings but your presence as a real on-chain participant.

## 3. Why the Channels Are Weighted the Way They Are

The five channels do not carry equal allocations, and the differences are not arbitrary. They encode a single principle: a channel’s share of the supply reflects the value of the contribution it asks for.

- Revenue-generating participation is given the most room and the least friction, because paying for real services is the most direct support a user can offer the protocol.

- Continuous data contribution is weighted more heavily than a one-time historical contribution, because an ongoing stream teaches an Agent more than a single snapshot ever can.

- Referral and holding-snapshot rewards are kept deliberately modest, because while they help the ecosystem grow, they ask less of the participant than data or revenue do.

Read together, the channels describe a hierarchy of contribution: the more real, sustained, and verifiable your participation, the larger the equity it earns. This is the same logic that runs through the entire economic model — behavior generates equity, and the equity earned is proportional to the behavior that was genuinely real.

## 4. What Happens to the LLAx You Earn

Once earned, LLAx has two uses, and they point the same way. You can spend it as fuel — using it to pay for select Agent and platform services — or you can convert it into LLA, the protocol’s value-bearing token. The conversion is one-directional: LLAx becomes LLA, never the reverse, and the LLAx is permanently burned in the process.

This matters for what your earned LLAx is worth over time. Because every conversion burns supply, the pool of LLAx shrinks as the ecosystem matures, while LLA — the token it converts into — accrues value from real protocol revenue through buybacks. Earning LLAx early, and converting it as the ecosystem grows, is therefore weighted toward those who contributed first. Your participation is not rewarded as a momentary payout but as an entry into the protocol’s long-term value layer.

## 5. Fair Participation

Every channel above rewards genuine participation, and the protocol protects that principle. Rewards are issued against data and behavior the protocol can independently verify — trading history is read directly through official exchange interfaces, never accepted as a user-supplied figure, and on-chain holdings are checked directly on-chain. Participation found to be fabricated — manufactured through batch accounts, falsified data, or simulated events — does not earn, and equity issued against it can be reclaimed.

None of this is meant to make honest participation harder. It exists so that the equity you earn through real contribution is not diluted by those who would counterfeit it. The value of LLAx rests entirely on the fact that it measures something real; safeguarding that is safeguarding what your own participation is worth.

## Summary

LLAx is how the LinkLayerAI ecosystem turns real participation into redeemable equity. You earn it by purchasing services, by contributing your trading data once and then continuously, by referring real users, and by being a present participant on-chain. The channels are weighted by the value of what they ask of you, the equity you earn flows one way toward the protocol’s long-term value, and the whole structure rests on participation being genuine. What you do in the ecosystem, done for real, becomes a share in what the ecosystem becomes.
